## Current RBI Monetary Policy Position

The Reserve Bank of India's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) held the repo rate at **6.50% per annum** at the October 2024 meeting, maintaining a neutral stance. This is the eighth consecutive hold after the rate peaked at 6.50% following a series of hikes that started in May 2022 to combat post-COVID inflation.

**Market expectation:** First 25 bps rate cut possible in February 2025 MPC meeting if food inflation moderates and US Fed signals rate reduction.

## What the Repo Rate Means for Bank Products

### Home Loans (Floating Rate — Repo-Linked)

Floating rate home loans are directly linked to the Repo Rate via the External Benchmark Lending Rate (EBLR) mechanism:

**EBLR = Repo Rate + Spread (set by the bank)**

Our current home loan EBLR structure:
- Repo Rate: 6.50%
- Bank Spread: 2.50% to 4.00% (based on credit profile)
- **Current effective home loan rates: 9.00% to 10.50% p.a.**

**Impact of a 25 bps Repo Rate cut (hypothetical):**
- New Repo Rate: 6.25%
- New effective home loan rate (for best-tier customers): 8.75% p.a.
- EMI reduction example: ₹50 lakh home loan over 20 years at 9.00% → EMI = ₹44,986; at 8.75% → EMI = ₹44,067. **Monthly saving: ₹919. Annual saving: ₹11,028.**

**RM Script:** "With the repo rate at 6.50% and a potential cut expected, this is a good time to take a floating rate home loan — you're positioned to benefit automatically when RBI cuts rates. Your EMI will drop without any paperwork."

### Personal Loans (MCLR-Linked)

Personal loan rates are linked to the bank's Marginal Cost of Funds-based Lending Rate (MCLR), which is updated monthly. MCLR is not directly tied to the Repo Rate but is influenced by it:

- Current 1-year MCLR: **8.50%** (as of October 2024)
- Personal loan rates: MCLR + 2.00% to 7.50% = **10.50% to 16.00%**

## RM Guidance for Rate-Sensitive Conversations

### When Customer Asks "Should I Wait for Rate Cuts?"

**For Home Loan:**
"The difference between 9.00% and 8.75% on your ₹50L loan is ₹919 per month — or about ₹11,000 per year. But if you wait 6 months hoping for the cut, you're paying ₹X in rent instead. Run the numbers — waiting is rarely cheaper than acting."

**For Personal Loan:**
"Personal loan rates are MCLR-linked, not directly Repo Rate-linked. Even after a Repo Rate cut, MCLR typically moves with a 1–2 quarter lag. The rate you get today is the rate available today."

**For FD:**
"FD rates are at a multi-year high. If you invest for 3 years now at 7%, and rates fall next year, you've locked in a great rate. This is the right window."
