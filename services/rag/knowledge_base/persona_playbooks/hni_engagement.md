# HNI Engagement Playbook — High Net Worth Individual Strategy

**Document Type:** Persona Playbook  
**Persona:** HNI (Assets Under Management above ₹50 Lakhs), Private Banking segment  
**Version:** 2024-Q4

---

## Persona Overview

HNI customers have built significant wealth and are highly aware of investment options. They are often time-constrained, have multiple banking relationships, and are sensitive to the quality of relationship management. A single misstep — poor product fit, irrelevant pitch, untimely contact — can trigger wealth migration to a competitor.

**Core insight:** HNI customers do not need the bank. The bank needs them. Every interaction must reflect this asymmetry.

---

## Engagement Principles

### 1. Never Lead with EMI Amount

Leading with "Your EMI would be ₹X per month" signals that you are treating an HNI like a retail borrower. This immediately reduces trust.

**Incorrect opening:** "Sir, the EMI would be only ₹45,000 per month for a 5-year term."

**Correct opening:** "This product structures your liquidity so your portfolio assets stay invested and compounding while the bank provides the capital bridge."

### 2. Always Anchor on Asset Creation

HNI customers think in terms of net worth, not monthly cash flow. Frame every product around what it does for their balance sheet:
- A loan is not a liability — it is leverage on an asset
- Insurance is not an expense — it is portfolio protection
- A fixed deposit is not savings — it is a liquidity buffer that earns above RBI repo rate

### 3. Offer Relationship Manager Home Visit

For any transaction above ₹50 lakhs or for account reviews, proactively offer to visit the customer at their home or office. Never make them come to the branch.

**Script:** "I'd like to schedule a visit to your office next week at your convenience — I have some portfolio insights and one product I think aligns with your current plans. Would Tuesday or Thursday work better?"

### 4. Reference Portfolio Performance Before Any Product Pitch

Before introducing any product, acknowledge the customer's existing relationship:
- Mention the return on their current FDs or mutual fund portfolio (if positive)
- Reference the value of their existing relationship: "Your savings account has averaged ₹X lakhs over the last 6 months — that's been working well"

Only then introduce the new product as a complement to what is already working.

### 5. Preferred Contact Time

HNI customers are typically available in the **evening between 7 PM and 9 PM** (post-work for professionals; post-market for business owners). Schedule all calls and visits during this window unless they specify otherwise.

**Do not:** Call between 10 AM and 2 PM on weekdays (typically their busiest work period).

## Escalation Path to Private Banking

When a customer's AUM reaches ₹40 lakhs (approaching ₹50 lakh threshold), initiate a warm introduction to the Private Banking team:

**Step 1:** Inform the customer that they are approaching Private Banking eligibility — frame it as an achievement, not a product pitch.

**Step 2:** Arrange a joint meeting with the Private Banker (no abrupt handover — the RM continues as point of contact).

**Step 3:** The Private Banker presents the enhanced service offering:
- Dedicated phone line (no IVR)
- Home visits
- Access to AIFs and pre-IPO opportunities
- Family office and estate planning services

**RM role post-upgrade:** Continues as the relationship anchor. The Private Banker handles investments; the RM handles transactional needs and loan requirements.

## Product Pitch Sequences (HNI Context)

### Sequence A: Promotion/Bonus Event
1. Acknowledge the achievement: "Congratulations on what sounds like a significant career milestone."
2. Pivot to portfolio: "With the additional liquidity, the question is — earn above inflation immediately, or deploy strategically?"
3. Options: Direct equity via PMS, NPS top-up for tax efficiency, or structured product for capital protection with returns
4. Never mention personal loan unless customer asks

### Sequence B: Business Expansion
1. Acknowledge the growth: "Expanding operations is exciting — capital allocation at this stage is critical."
2. Lead with Working Capital: "We can structure a working capital facility so your own capital stays in the business as equity."
3. Overlay: Private Banking can arrange structured credit facilities with flexible drawdown

### Sequence C: Real Estate Purchase
1. Acknowledge status: "Acquiring premium real estate is both an investment and a lifestyle decision."
2. Lead with Loan Against Securities (LAS): "Rather than liquidating your portfolio, you can use it as collateral — your investments keep growing while the bank provides the home loan capital."
3. Alternatively, a standard home loan with top private banking rates

## Communication Tone Guidelines

- **Formal but personal:** Use first name only if the customer has explicitly invited it
- **Concise:** No more than 3 key points per message or call — HNIs do not have patience for lengthy pitches
- **Written preference:** High-quality PDF summaries over WhatsApp text; email with charts and graphs for investment proposals
- **Avoid:** Jargon, acronyms without explanation, sales pressure language
- **Always confirm:** Repeat back any agreed action item in writing within 2 hours

---

## Red Flags (Immediate RM Action Required)

- Customer requests a summary of all FD and investment balances (potential exit signal)
- Large IMPS/NEFT transfer to a non-regular account
- Customer mentions a competitor's rate or product offering
- Reduced WhatsApp/call responsiveness over 3+ weeks
- Complaint about any bank process — even minor — from an HNI
