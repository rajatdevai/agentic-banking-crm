# Startup Founder Engagement Playbook

**Document Type:** Persona Playbook  
**Persona:** Startup Founders, Early-Stage Entrepreneurs, Business Owners with VC/Angel Backing  
**Version:** 2024-Q4

---

## Persona Overview

Startup founders operate in a fundamentally different financial context than salaried professionals. Their income is often irregular, their business is in a growth phase, and they are highly intelligent about capital efficiency. They see every banking interaction through the lens of burn rate, runway, and return on capital.

**Core insight:** Founders are allergic to rigid, inflexible financial products. They need banking partners who understand business cycles, not just credit scores.

---

## Engagement Principles

### 1. Acknowledge Business Cycles — Never Compare to Salaried Benchmarks

A founder who earned ₹10L last month and ₹5L this month is not inconsistent — they are running a business. Do not apply salaried-customer logic.

**Incorrect:** "Your average monthly income is only ₹7 lakhs, which limits your loan eligibility."

**Correct:** "Based on your quarterly cash flow pattern, we can structure a facility that works with your business's natural rhythm."

### 2. Lead with Working Capital Flexibility

Founders think in terms of runway. The first product conversation should always be about operational liquidity:

- **Overdraft facility** (current account-linked): Best first product — pay interest only on what you draw. Aligns perfectly with variable cash flow.
- **Working capital term loan**: For specific growth investments — new team, equipment, geographic expansion.
- **Invoice discounting**: If the business has B2B receivables — convert outstanding invoices to immediate cash.

**Never lead with:** Personal loans, FDs, or retail investment products in the first conversation.

### 3. Offer Overdraft Before Term Loan

An overdraft facility is almost always the right first product for a founder:
- Flexible drawdown — draw ₹50L one month, repay ₹30L next month
- Interest only on outstanding balance — cost-efficient during low-burn months
- No pre-closure penalty — pay off when funding round closes

**Pitch:** "Think of it as a credit line that flexes with your business. When you need capital fast, it's there. When you have surplus, you pay it down and your interest cost drops immediately."

### 4. Avoid Comparing to Salaried Benchmarks

Never say: "Typically a salaried person at your income level would qualify for X." Founders will disengage immediately.

Frame every parameter in business context: "Based on your ARR growth trajectory, the facility can scale as the business scales."

### 5. Reference Sector-Specific Schemes

Several government and bank schemes are specifically designed for startups:

| Scheme | Benefit | Eligibility |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| CGTMSE | Collateral-free loans up to ₹2 Crore with government guarantee | MSME-registered startups |
| Stand-Up India | ₹10L to ₹1Cr for SC/ST/women entrepreneurs | First-time business owners |
| SIDBI SMILE | Soft loans for manufacturing startups | Manufacturing/tech |
| Mudra Tarun | ₹5L to ₹10L working capital | Early-stage micro-enterprises |
| Startup India Credit Guarantee | 80% credit guarantee on term loans | DPIIT-recognised startups |

**RM Action:** Always check if the founder's startup is DPIIT-registered — it unlocks significant financing advantages.

## Cash Flow Sensitive Communication

Founders are acutely aware of costs. Communicate the economics of every product clearly:

**Interest rate framing:** Don't quote annual rate. Quote monthly cost: "On a ₹50L overdraft, if you draw ₹20L for 20 days, your interest cost is approximately ₹33,000 — roughly 0.17% of the draw amount."

**Processing fee framing:** "One-time fee. Over the 3-year term of the facility, it amortises to ₹X per month — less than one coffee a day."

**Pre-closure benefit:** "If your Series A closes early, you can repay the full amount with zero penalty. No lock-in — unlike a traditional term loan."

## Investment Products for Founders

Once the primary banking relationship is established, the following investment products resonate with founders:

1. **Liquid Mutual Funds:** Better yield than current account on idle treasury cash (typically 6.5–7.5% p.a.) — pitch as "treasury management"
2. **Sovereign Gold Bonds:** Tax-efficient store of value for founders who want inflation protection
3. **NPS (Founder as Employee of Own Company):** Employer contribution up to 10% of salary tax-deductible under 80CCD(2) — especially relevant if founder draws salary
4. **Term Insurance:** High coverage, low premium — founders often underinsured. Business continuity risk for investors.

## Red Flags (Immediate RM Action Required)

- **Salary account shows no credit for 2+ months:** Potential distress — call immediately, frame as a check-in
- **GST filing drops sharply:** Revenue may be declining
- **Requests to break FD:** Liquidity crunch; offer overdraft as alternative
- **Multiple credit enquiries from other banks:** Competitor evaluation; proactively offer better terms
- **Asks for reference to legal team:** Potential acquisition/shutdown — high-value relationship conversation needed

---

## Tone and Communication Style

- **Peer-to-peer:** Treat the founder as a business partner, not a retail customer
- **WhatsApp is acceptable** but keep messages professional — bullet points, not paragraphs
- **Data-driven:** Back recommendations with numbers. Founders trust data over persuasion.
- **Respect their time:** Keep calls under 15 minutes. Send a one-pager summary after any call.
- **Avoid:** Banking jargon ("NPA", "FOIR", "DCCO") without explanation
