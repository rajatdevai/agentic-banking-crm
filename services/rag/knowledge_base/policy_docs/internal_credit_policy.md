## Credit Scoring Cutoffs

### Automated Decisioning Thresholds

| Score Band | Decision | Action |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Internal Score 750+ (CIBIL 750+) | **Auto-approve** | Instant disbursal for pre-approved customers |
| Internal Score 650–749 (CIBIL 700–749) | **Conditional approve** | Verify income documents; RM review before sanction |
| Internal Score 550–649 (CIBIL 650–699) | **Manual underwriting required** | Credit analyst review; additional documents; possible co-borrower |
| Internal Score below 550 (CIBIL below 650) | **Decline** | System-generated decline letter within 24 hours |

**Note:** Internal Score combines CIBIL score (60% weight), bureau tradeline behaviour (20%), relationship depth with our bank (10%), and employment stability (10%).

### Override Authority
| Override Level | Authority | Maximum Amount |
|---------------|-----------|----------------|
| RM level | None — cannot override system decision | — |
| Branch Manager | Can approve Conditional cases to Approve | Up to ₹25 lakhs |
| Zonal Credit Head | Manual underwriting cases | Up to ₹2 Crore |
| Chief Credit Officer | All cases | No limit |

## Sector-Specific Exposure Limits

The bank maintains portfolio-level sector concentration limits to manage systemic risk:

| Sector | Maximum Exposure (% of total retail book) |
|--------|------------------------------------------|
| Real Estate (mortgage-backed) | 35% |
| Personal Loans (unsecured) | 20% |
| Education Loans | 8% |
| Auto Loans | 12% |
| MSME / Working Capital | 15% |
| Gold Loans | 5% |
| Other / Diversified | 5% |

**RM Action:** When sector limit is near (within 5% of cap), the Credit team issues a "Selective Approval" notice to RMs. Do not pipeline new applications in that sector until limit resets (quarterly).

## Triggers for Manual Underwriting Review

Any of the following flags escalate a case to Credit Analyst review regardless of internal score:

### Automatic Escalation Triggers
1. **Derogatory bureau entries:** Any settled account, written-off amount, or Wilful Defaulter flag in the past 7 years
2. **Multiple active enquiries:** More than 4 bureau enquiries in last 90 days (indicates credit shopping)
3. **High obligation ratio:** Total EMI obligations exceed 50% of gross monthly income before proposed loan
4. **Income mismatch:** Declared income differs from bank statement average by more than 20%
5. **Employment gap:** Gap in employment of more than 3 months in the past 2 years
6. **Restructured account:** Any account that was restructured under COVID moratorium or OTS settlement
7. **Loan against property:** Where property is already mortgaged to another lender (sub-lien situation)
8. **Business loan with personal guarantee:** Personal loan application from same customer — evaluate total exposure
9. **NRI accounts:** All NRI/NRO-linked loan applications require manual review (FEMA compliance)
10. **Politically Exposed Person (PEP):** Any identified PEP requires compliance clearance

## Post-Disbursal Monitoring Requirements

### Early Warning System (EWS) Triggers

Once a loan is disbursed, the following behaviours trigger EWS alerts:

| EWS Level | Signal | RM Action |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Green | Account performing; no alerts | Regular monitoring |
| Amber | 1 missed EMI OR 30-day delay | RM to call within 48 hours; understand reason |
| Red | 2 consecutive missed EMIs OR 60-day delay | Escalate to Recovery team; RM files SMA-1 report |
| Critical | 90 days overdue | NPA classification; legal notice initiated |

### Loan Utilisation Verification
- Personal loans above ₹10 lakhs: End-use certificate required within 30 days
- Business loans: Quarterly stock statements and revenue declarations
- Education loans: University fee receipt mandatory for each disbursement tranche

## Prohibited Practices

The following are strictly prohibited under internal policy:

1. Sanctioning a loan application where the RM has a personal relationship or financial interest in the applicant
2. Waiving documentation requirements beyond delegated authority
3. Antedating documents or processing applications with incomplete KYC
4. Accepting informal payments, gifts above ₹500 value, or benefits of any kind from applicants
5. Sharing bureau reports or customer financial data with third parties without documented customer consent
