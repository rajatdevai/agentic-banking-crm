# RBI Fair Practices Code & Lending Guidelines

**Document Type:** Regulatory Policy  
**Version:** 2024 — Based on RBI Master Circular on Fair Practices Code  
**Applicable to:** All retail lending activities, personal loans, home loans, education loans  
**Regulatory Authority:** Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

---

## Overview

The Reserve Bank of India's Fair Practices Code (FPC) for banks and NBFCs establishes minimum standards for transparent, fair, and non-discriminatory lending. All RMs and loan officers are required to adhere to this code in every customer interaction. Non-compliance is subject to regulatory action and internal disciplinary proceedings.

---

## KYC (Know Your Customer) Requirements

### Mandatory KYC Documents

Every customer must complete KYC before any loan is sanctioned or disbursed:

**Identity Proof (any one):**
- Aadhaar Card (UID-linked)
- Passport
- Voter ID Card
- Driving Licence
- PAN Card (mandatory for all loans above ₹50,000)

**Address Proof (any one):**
- Aadhaar Card
- Utility bill (electricity/telephone — not older than 3 months)
- Bank account statement with address
- Registered sale/rent agreement

### Video KYC (V-KYC)
- Available for salaried individuals with Aadhaar-linked mobile number
- Reduces physical branch visit requirement
- Not available for self-employed without GST registration

### KYC Refresh Cycle
- High-risk customers: Every 2 years
- Medium-risk: Every 8 years
- Low-risk: Every 10 years
- **Expired KYC blocks fresh loan disbursement** — RM must alert customer to refresh before application

## Interest Rate Disclosure Rules

RBI mandates full transparency in interest rate communication:

### Mandatory Disclosures at Sanction
1. **Annualised percentage rate (APR):** Must include all fees, not just the base interest rate
2. **Processing fees:** Must be disclosed upfront — cannot add hidden charges post-sanction
3. **Penal charges:** Must be disclosed at application stage; maximum penal rate limited to **2% per month** on overdue amounts
4. **Rate reset:** For floating rate loans, the benchmark (Repo Rate/MCLR), the spread, and the reset frequency must be clearly disclosed
5. **Pre-closure charges:** Disclosure mandatory. Floating rate retail loans: zero pre-closure penalty per RBI circular dated 27-Aug-2019

### Annualised Percentage Rate (APR) Formula
APR includes:
- Nominal interest rate
- Processing fee (amortised over loan tenure)
- Insurance premium (if mandatory)
- Legal/valuation charges (for secured loans)

**Example:** Personal Loan at 12% p.a. + 1.5% processing fee on ₹5L over 36 months → APR ≈ 13.8% p.a.

### Communication Standards
- All loan documents must be in English AND the regional language if requested
- Sanction letter must be issued before disbursal — customer's written acceptance required
- Loan agreement must list all terms and conditions — no oral commitments count

---

## Maximum Interest Rate Rules

1. No bank may charge interest rates that are **usurious** (RBI benchmark: excessive relative to risk)
2. For microfinance and NBFC lending: hard cap exists; banks have risk-based pricing guidelines
3. Rate discrimination based on gender, religion, caste, or region is **prohibited**
4. Rate must be disclosed to customer in writing — verbal-only rate communication is non-compliant

## Grievance Redressal Mandate

### Internal Escalation Path

All banks must have a published grievance redressal mechanism:

**Level 1:** Branch Customer Service (resolve within **3 working days**)  
**Level 2:** Zonal Customer Service Officer (resolve within **7 working days**)  
**Level 3:** Principal Nodal Officer at Head Office (resolve within **15 working days**)  
**Level 4:** Banking Ombudsman (if unresolved after 30 days from Level 1 complaint)

### Ombudsman Scheme (RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme 2021)
- Customer can lodge complaint online at CMS portal (cms.rbi.org.in)
- No fee for filing complaint
- Ombudsman's award is binding on the bank up to ₹20 lakh
- RM must inform customers of their right to approach Ombudsman at the first instance of any complaint

### Response Timeline SLAs (Mandatory)
| Complaint Type | Maximum Resolution Time |
|----------------|------------------------|
| ATM / Digital transactions | 7 calendar days |
| Loan-related disputes | 15 working days |
| Account closure / portability | 30 working days |
| Insurance claim linked to loan | 15 working days from insurer's decision |

## Fair Practices in Loan Recovery

1. **No intimidation or harassment** of customers or guarantors during recovery
2. Recovery agents must carry authorisation letter from the bank
3. Recovery contact hours: **7 AM to 7 PM only** — no night calls
4. Contact at workplace not permitted if customer has stated objection
5. **Third-party mediation:** Customer has right to request restructuring before account is classified as NPA

---

## RM Compliance Obligations

Every RM must:
1. Never make oral commitments on rate, tenure, or charges that differ from the sanctioned terms
2. Disclose all associated products (insurance, credit card) as optional — bundling is non-compliant
3. Not create urgency pressure to force immediate acceptance of terms
4. Retain customer consent records for all digital product authorisations (7-year minimum)
5. Report any suspected mis-selling or complaint immediately to the Compliance Officer
