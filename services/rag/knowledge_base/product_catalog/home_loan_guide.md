## Overview

Our Home Loan product helps customers purchase, construct, or renovate residential property in India. Available for salaried and self-employed customers with competitive interest rates, flexible tenures up to 30 years, and integration with the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) subsidy scheme for first-time buyers.

## Eligibility Criteria

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Age | 21 years (minimum) to 70 years (at loan maturity) |
| Minimum Net Monthly Salary | ₹30,000 (salaried) / ₹40,000 (self-employed) |
| Employment Stability | Minimum 2 years in current organisation |
| CIBIL Score | 650 and above (700+ preferred for best rates) |
| Maximum Debt-to-Income | 55% including proposed home loan EMI |
| Loan Tenure | 5 years to 30 years |
| Minimum Loan Amount | ₹10,00,000 |
| Maximum Loan Amount | ₹10,00,00,000 (₹10 Crore) |

## Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratios

LTV determines the maximum loan amount as a percentage of the property's market value:

| Property Value | Maximum LTV | Minimum Down Payment |
|---------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Up to ₹30,00,000 | 90% | 10% of property value |
| ₹30,00,001 to ₹75,00,000 | 80% | 20% of property value |
| Above ₹75,00,000 | 75% | 25% of property value |

**Example:** For a property valued at ₹1 Crore, the maximum loan amount is ₹75,00,000. The customer must arrange the remaining ₹25,00,000 as a down payment from their own funds.

> **Important:** LTV is applied to the lower of (a) the registered sale price and (b) the bank's internal property valuation.

## Interest Rate Structure

### Floating Rate (Linked to Repo Rate)

Current Repo Rate: **6.50%** (as per latest RBI Monetary Policy Committee decision)

| Customer Segment | Spread over Repo Rate | Effective Rate |
|-----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| CIBIL 750+ Salaried | Repo + 2.50% | 9.00% p.a. |
| CIBIL 700-749 Salaried | Repo + 3.00% | 9.50% p.a. |
| CIBIL 650-699 Any | Repo + 4.00% | 10.50% p.a. |
| Self-employed (CIBIL 700+) | Repo + 3.25% | 9.75% p.a. |
| Women co-applicant benefit | Additional –0.10% | Applied on above |

### Fixed Rate Option (2-Year Lock-In)

Available at 9.75% to 11.00% p.a. depending on credit profile. Fixed period is 2 years only; converts to floating rate thereafter. **Not recommended in a declining rate environment** — floating rate is better when RBI rate cuts are expected.

## Co-Applicant Rules

- **Mandatory co-applicant:** When property is jointly owned or when primary applicant's income is insufficient
- **Eligible co-applicants:** Spouse, parents, adult children (earning)
- **Income clubbing:** Co-applicant income can be clubbed to enhance loan eligibility
- **CIBIL requirement:** Co-applicant's CIBIL also evaluated — lower of the two scores affects pricing
- **Non-earning co-applicant:** Allowed for legal purposes (joint property ownership) but income not clubbed

## PMAY Subsidy (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana)

First-time homebuyers may qualify for interest subsidy under PMAY Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS):

| Income Category | Annual Household Income | Subsidy Rate | Max Eligible Loan | Subsidy Amount |
|----------------|------------------------|--------------|------------------|----------------|
| EWS | Up to ₹3,00,000 | 6.50% | ₹6,00,000 | Up to ₹2,67,280 |
| LIG | ₹3,00,001 to ₹6,00,000 | 6.50% | ₹6,00,000 | Up to ₹2,67,280 |
| MIG-I | ₹6,00,001 to ₹12,00,000 | 4.00% | ₹9,00,000 | Up to ₹2,35,068 |
| MIG-II | ₹12,00,001 to ₹18,00,000 | 3.00% | ₹12,00,000 | Up to ₹2,30,156 |

**Eligibility conditions for PMAY:**
- First-time home purchase — applicant (or spouse) should not own any residential property
- Property size limits apply: EWS/LIG ≤ 60 sq.m carpet; MIG-I ≤ 160 sq.m; MIG-II ≤ 200 sq.m
- Aadhar linking mandatory for subsidy disbursement

**RM Action:** Always check PMAY eligibility for customers in MIG-I and MIG-II income bands who are first-time buyers. The subsidy effectively reduces the net interest cost significantly.

## Floating vs Fixed Rate Guidance

**When to recommend Floating Rate:**
- Customer has a long tenure (15+ years) — average out rate fluctuations over time
- Current RBI stance is neutral or accommodative — rate cuts expected
- Customer is financially stable and can absorb EMI changes

**When to recommend Fixed Rate (2-year lock-in):**
- Customer needs EMI certainty for budgeting (e.g., fixed corporate salary)
- Rates are at historically low levels and likely to rise
- Loan tenure is short (under 10 years) — fixed-rate certainty matters more

**Current Guidance (as of system design):** With repo rate at 6.50% and RBI signalling neutral stance, floating rate is marginally preferred for tenures above 15 years.

| Charge | Amount |

| Pre-closure (floating rate) | NIL (RBI directive) |

## RM Conversation Guidance

- **Lead with stability:** "Your home is your most secure investment — the EMI you pay today is rent you stop paying forever."
- **PMAY hook:** For first-time buyers, always open with "Did you know you may qualify for a government subsidy up to ₹2.35 lakhs?"
- **Co-applicant advantage:** Mention that adding a co-applicant (spouse) improves eligibility and gets the women borrower discount
- **Rate transparency:** Show the Repo Rate connection — "Your rate moves with RBI. When RBI cuts rates, your EMI drops automatically."
- **Pre-closure reassurance:** Floating rate has zero pre-closure penalty — this removes the psychological barrier for customers who fear being locked in
